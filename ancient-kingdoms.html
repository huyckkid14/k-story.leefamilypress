First Kingdoms

A journey through Korea’s earliest peoples, myths, kingdoms, and heroes—based on the real chapters of K-Story: History, Legends & Songs of Korea, Book 1.

Summary of Book 1: Ancient Kingdoms

Book 1: Ancient Kingdoms guides readers through the earliest eras of Korean history, beginning with the first people of the Korean Peninsula and the rise of Ancient Joseon. From the origin legend of Dangun to the fall of Joseon in 108 BCE, the book blends archaeology, myth, and historical memory in a way that feels alive and story-driven.

After the fall of Joseon, the book explores the Samhan peoples, Buyeo, Dongye, and the shifting world that shaped the birth of new leaders and clans. Readers then journey into the founding of the first great kingdoms: Goguryeo, Baekje, and Silla.

Through chapters such as The Boy Who Could Outshoot the Sky, The Nine Eggs of the Sky, and the stories of rulers, warriors, traders, and scholars, the book paints a rich portrait of Korea’s beginnings. These tales are grounded in real historical timelines such as:

  • c. 8000–1500 BCE: Early pottery and coastal village life
  • 2333 BCE: Legendary founding of Ancient Joseon by Dangun
  • 37 BCE: Jumong founds Goguryeo
  • 18 BCE: Onjo founds Baekje
  • 57 BCE: Hyeokgeose Park becomes the first king of Silla

By combining myth, legend, and archaeological evidence, the book helps young readers understand how Korea’s earliest cultures grew into kingdoms that shaped thousands of years of history.
