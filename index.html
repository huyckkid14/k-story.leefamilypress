Based on archaeological discoveries and timeless works like Samguk Sagi and Samguk Yusa, K-Story brings Korea’s ancient past to life with both accuracy and heart.

Neither a dry academic text nor a simplified picture book, it fills a rare middle ground – a chapter book of Korean history in English.

It reads like a story, yet teaches like history. Engaging for young readers, thoughtful for families and classrooms, K-Story turns Korea’s earliest myths and records into living, memorable tales.

Where Korea’s Earliest Heroes Come to Life

K-Story: History, Legends & Songs of Korea – Book 1: Ancient Kingdoms invites readers into the dawn of Korean history – a time when kings, generals, and scholars built a nation guided by courage, wisdom, and faith.

These are the stories of real people who dreamed, led, and sacrificed, shaping the values that still define Korea today.

Not distant myths, but living memories – retold with warmth and clarity for a new generation of readers.

The First K-pop: Ancient Songs That Traveled Beyond Kingdoms

Long before streaming or stadiums, Korea had its own kind of K-pop – songs everyone knew and loved.

From river paths to mountain villages, melodies spread from voice to voice, carried by emotion and memory rather than machines.

Some even traveled to distant lands, becoming the hits of the ancient world.

In K-Story: History, Legends & Songs of Korea, these early songs return in their story settings – living echoes that once united hearts and kingdoms.

The First K-dramas: Love Stories That Still Move the Heart

In every era, Koreans have told stories of love that shaped both lives and kingdoms.

These were tales of devotion that defied class and expectation – of feelings so powerful they changed the course of history.

In K-Story: History, Legends & Songs of Korea, these romances unfold within their historical moments, revealing how love, loyalty, and courage have always been at the heart of Korea’s story.

The first K-dramas were not filmed, but sung, remembered, and passed down – stories that still move hearts across centuries.
